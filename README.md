10. Filing Principles


All OSTA filings follow five administrative principles.


Principle One: Preserve the Record


A dispute cannot be audited if the record is missing.


Filings should include evidence, timestamps, links, screenshots, model outputs, public posts, article references, profile snapshots, or other receipts.


Principle Two: Separate Satire from Claim


The Office permits satire.


The Office requires clarity.


Mascot custody may be comedic, but hallucinated claims must be identified carefully.


Principle Three: Do Not Confuse Representation with Ownership


A public profile, mascot reference, AI-generated summary, or semantic filing does not create ownership.


Representation can be observed, disputed, corrected, or audited.


Ownership requires separate authority.


Principle Four: Track Drift Over Time


The significance of a claim often appears only after comparison.


A single summary may be noise.


A repeated claim may be drift.


A coordinated change may be manipulation.


A pre-manipulation baseline may become evidence.


Principle Five: Peace Was Offered Multiple Times


Where applicable, the filing should indicate whether the affected party was offered acknowledgment, correction, release, or public off-ramp before escalation.


This phrase functions as both doctrine and checksum.
